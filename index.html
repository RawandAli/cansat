Terraforming

Terraforming is the process of identifying and transforming a planet so that it can sustain human life. Our satellite aims to achieve a miniscule yet important portion of the first part of terraforming, locating an adequate planet. To understand how much terraforming must be done before life can be sustained, some factors about the environment must be measured. This includes atmospheric humidity, temperature, pressure and most importantly soil pH. Quantitatively measuring these factors can be enough to make a basic prognosis on the feasibility of terraforming the planet.

Project

Our lander style satellite has a multitude of sensors that allow it to measure these parameters. This measurement is achieved using a barometer, thermometer, humidity sensor and pH probe. The pH probe is used to measure the pH of a sample that is obtained by drilling the surface of the planet. This approach allows our lander to sample the pH of a vast variety of materials. After the measurements are conducted, the information is transmitted back to the ground station where a comprehensive analysis can be made.
